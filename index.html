Unbranded Campaigns

Unbranded Campaign Governance

Category analysis, color audit, worldwide governance standard, and operational infrastructure delivered

Unbranded campaigns — known within the industry as disease-education campaigns — are a significant communications category in pharmaceutical marketing. They reach patients and healthcare professionals in spaces where branded promotion is not available, and at a scale that requires consistent visual and regulatory standards. This program established the governance architecture to operate that category with coherence across all markets.

Context

Unbranded campaigns were produced across commercial, medical affairs, and corporate functions, each sourcing its own creative direction. The agency of record had mapped the landscape as a spectrum — ranging from masterbrand materials at one end to fully unbranded campaigns at the other, with graduated ratios of branded-to-unbranded visual identity at each stage:

Findings

A category-wide analysis mapped visual identity across markets and therapeutic areas, measuring how unbranded campaign palettes related to branded product palettes within the same disease categories. In several therapeutic areas, unbranded campaigns drew from the same primary and secondary color families as branded product materials — placing them in close visual proximity. In a regulated industry, color distance between branded and unbranded materials is a compliance consideration. The proposed direction: assign each therapeutic area a dedicated color family from the tertiary palette so that unbranded campaigns are visually distinct from branded materials by design, not by discretion.

Worldwide Standard

Building on the agency’s masterbrand/unbranded spectrum, this program introduced a guidelines flexibility spectrum to govern how the full set of brand standards — design, visual identity, and application guidelines, not only color — apply to local market unbranded campaigns at each point along that continuum. Campaigns closer to the masterbrand adhere to stricter corporate standards; campaigns further along the unbranded continuum have greater latitude for regional and therapeutic-area adaptation — within the governance framework.
