Corporate Brand Process Redesign
A company-wide rebrand created simultaneous demand for brand guidance across every function and market.
Restructured the corporate brand request process. Audited incoming requests by type and frequency across logo usage, creative services, photo and video, unbranded campaign review, and content library access. Found that the majority were repetitive. Unbranded Campaigns
Unbranded Campaign Governance
Category analysis, color audit, worldwide governance standard, and operational infrastructure delivered
Unbranded campaigns, known within the industry as disease-education campaigns, are a significant communications category in pharmaceutical marketing. They reach patients and healthcare professionals in spaces where branded promotion is not available, and at a scale that requires consistent visual and regulatory standards. This program established the governance architecture to operate that category with coherence across all markets.
Context
Unbranded campaigns were produced across commercial, medical affairs, and corporate functions, each sourcing its own creative direction. The category operated without a unified governance standard at the enterprise level. The agency of record had mapped the landscape as a spectrum, ranging from masterbrand materials at one end to fully unbranded campaigns at the other, with graduated ratios of branded-to-unbranded visual identity at each stage:

Findings
A category-wide analysis mapped visual identity across markets and therapeutic areas, measuring how unbranded campaign palettes related to branded product palettes within the same disease categories. In several therapeutic areas, unbranded campaigns drew from the same primary and secondary color families as branded product materials, placing them in close visual proximity. In a regulated industry, color distance between branded and unbranded materials is a compliance consideration. The proposed direction: assign each therapeutic area a dedicated color family from the tertiary palette so that unbranded campaigns are visually distinct from branded materials by design, not by discretion.

Campaign Development
Corporate Marketing Campaign: Concept Development
The primary concept positioned the company's science as a continuous, emotionally legible story, accessible to a general audience without sacrificing accuracy or scientific credibility. A five-act narrative structure traced the journey of a molecule from laboratory to patient, designed to make abstract research tangible across a general audience spanning patients, investors, healthcare professionals, and the public. The architecture was built to scale: the same narrative could be expressed as a short-form social asset, a long-form film, a large-scale experiential installation at scientific congresses, or an out-of-home advertising program at major transit hubs.
